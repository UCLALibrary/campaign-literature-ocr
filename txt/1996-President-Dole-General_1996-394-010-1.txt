“There’s not a dime’s worth of difference; both political parties
are alike.”

“| vote for the person, not the party.”
“If I’m anything, I’m Independent.”

A lot of Americans feel this way today and certainly, the voters’
instinct for electing the right candidate at the right time,
regardless of party, has proven itself time and again.

Few would question the importance or the power of the Independent
vote in deciding closely contested elections. And it is true that
both parties are alike in many respects.

However, to ignore a candidate's party affiliation is to miss out
on one of the great advantages of the two-party system. The
very reason that a candidate is listed as a REPUBLICAN or a
DEMOCRAT is that he or she subscribes to the basic political
philosophy for which that party stands...
